Started this paper at Addenbrooke’s

Think of the relationship between infrastructure and technological wave.

Each technological wave, as it begins, requires an coordination of some sort to get the wave started. The technology has the potential of delivering a Pareto improvement. Infrastructure is where a decentralised process fails to deliver a potential Pareto improvement.

conversation with stan and lois. 24 July 2022, 14:07

There is a time component to the infrastructure, i.e., assets that cannot be maintained over a long period unless there is a sufficient arrangements made for looking after it. Hence companies tend to distinguish between investments that maximises the value of company versus investments that maintain the infrastructure and ensure its quality over a long span.

Papers notes

  • users pay a user fee
  • Depreciation: difficult to calculate
  • Benefits intangible and hence difficult to capture, hence difficult to relate and evaluate infrastructure impact on its goals/objectives
